BrowseDNS.net

Domain Name Servers (DNS) are the Internet's equivalent of a phone book. They maintain a directory of domain names and translate them to Internet Protocol (IP) addresses. This is necessary because, although domain names are easy for people to remember, computers or machines, access websites based on IP addresses.

BrowseDNS.net provides tools to help analyze and validate your DNS configurations. As well we provide information on how to create DNS records of different types.

Check out our docs on how to format your DNS records. We currently have information on IP address records (A, AAAA), mail records (MX), and certificate authority authorization records (CAA).
